# Easiest Photo Slideshow
This is a single HTML file that will give you a quick, yet feature filled, option for a collage style photoslide show without the need of downloading apps, using Google or Microsoft apps and works offline. 

A beautiful, automatic photo slideshow that runs right in your web browser — no apps to install, no accounts to create. Just open one file, pick your photo folder, and watch the show.

---

## ✨ What It Does

- Displays your photos in fun, random layouts — side by side, big and small, full screen, and more
- Smoothly transitions between screens with fades, slides, zooms, and flips
- Loops forever on its own — set it and forget it
- Works on any laptop or desktop connected to a TV

---

## 💻 What You Need

- A laptop or desktop computer
- **Microsoft Edge Browser** is fine OR **Google Chrome** browser (free at [google.com/chrome](https://google.com/chrome))
- Your photos in a folder on your computer
- An HDMI cable to connect your laptop to the TV *(optional — works on your laptop screen too)*

That's it. Nothing to download or install beyond Chrome.

---

## 🚀 How to Get Started

### Step 1 — Put your photos in a folder

Gather all the photos you want in the slideshow and put them in one folder on your computer. You can name it anything you like, such as **"Graduation Photos"**.

> Photos can be JPG, PNG, or most common image formats. Hundreds of photos work just fine.

---

### Step 2 — Open the slideshow file

Find the file called **`graduation_slideshow.html`** and double-click it. It will open in you ar browser (or right-click and Open With to choose what browser).
You will see the loading screen with the options and folder selection button. 

---

### Step 3 — Adjust your settings *(optional)*

Before the show starts, you can customize how it looks:

| Setting | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Max photos per screen** | How many photos show at once — from 1 (full screen) up to 6 (4 is the sweet spot) | 
| **Slide speed** | How long each screen stays up — drag the slider from 3 to 20 seconds |
| **Grid styles** | The different layout styles used — check the ones you like |
| **Transitions** | The effects between screens — check the ones you like |

All boxes are checked by default, which means the show picks randomly from everything. You can uncheck anything you don't want.

---

### Step 4 — Choose your photo folder and start

Click the **"Choose Photo Folder & Start"** button.

A window will pop up — navigate to your **"Graduation Photos"** folder (or whereever your photos are) and click **Open** (or **Upload** depending on your computer).

Depending on how many photos this step may take a minute... be patient, there's a status under the button while loading.

The show will start automatically. 🎉

---

### Step 5 — Go fullscreen on the TV

Once the show is running, press **F11** on your keyboard (Some laptops might be **Fn + F11** to go fullscreen. 
OR you can hit **Alt + F** on Edge and Chrome to pull up the menu, the button in the Zoom line will be fullscreen on both browsers. 

If your laptop is connected to the TV with an HDMI cable, make sure your TV is selected as the display — the slideshow will fill the whole screen.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**My photos look stretched or cut off — is that normal?**
No — each photo is shown in full with no cropping. Dark bars may appear on the sides or top/bottom of portrait photos displayed in a wide tile, similar to how movies look on TV. That's intentional so nothing gets cut off.

**Can I add or remove photos after I start?**
You'd need to refresh the page and choose the folder again. It's easiest to have your photo folder finalized before you start.

**The show stopped — what happened?**
Check that your laptop's power settings aren't putting it to sleep. On Windows, search **"Power & sleep settings"** and set Sleep to **Never** while plugged in. On Mac, go to **System Settings → Battery → Prevent automatic sleeping**.

**How do I stop the show?**
Press `Escape` or close the Chrome window.

**Does this need the internet?**
No. Once Chrome is open and your photos are loaded, everything runs locally on your computer. No internet required.

## 💛 Tips for a Great Show

- **Use good lighting.** Photos taken in decent light will look much better on a big TV screen than dark or blurry ones.
- **Mix it up.** A variety of candid shots, group photos, and close-ups makes the show more interesting than all posed photos.
- **Test it first.** Run the show on your laptop the day before the party to make sure everything looks right.
- **Keep the laptop plugged in.** Running a slideshow all evening will drain your battery fast.
- **Bump up the speed.** If you have hundreds of photos, a shorter slide time (3–5 seconds) keeps things moving and energetic.
